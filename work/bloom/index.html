Bloom — Redesigning a wellness tracker from the ground up

RoleLead UX Designer
Duration14 weeks
PlatformiOS & Android
Overview

Bloom came to me with a retention problem. Users were downloading the app and dropping off within the first three days — never making it past the onboarding to actually build a habit. The core product was solid, but the experience felt clinical and overwhelming.

My goal was to redesign the onboarding and home experience to feel lighter, more personal, and actually motivating — without losing the depth that existing power users loved.

Process
01

Research & Audit

Conducted 8 user interviews and reviewed session recordings. Mapped where users were dropping off in the existing onboarding — 62% left during the goal setting step.

02

Reframing the Problem

The old flow asked users to commit to everything upfront. We shifted to progressive disclosure — let them start with one habit and earn complexity over time.

03

Wireframes & Prototyping

Built 3 lo-fi flows in Figma and tested them with 12 participants. The "one habit to start" concept tested significantly better on perceived ease and motivation.

04

Visual Design & Handoff

Refined the visual language — softer tones, generous spacing, micro-animations for check-ins. Delivered a full Figma component set with dev-ready specs.
